    My role

  • Motion system design for large-scale LED displays
  • Animation framework optimized for varying viewing distances
  • Collaboration with OneMethod and Scotiabank

Scotiabank Arena's exterior LED displays required a motion graphics system that functioned as a landmark installation in Toronto's downtown core, seen by millions of annual visitors and residents. The animations needed to work as ambient brand presence across varying viewing distances and lighting conditions—from pedestrians at street level to drivers on the Gardiner Expressway.

The system remains in continuous use today, demonstrating its durability as infrastructure rather than campaign-based creative. This was work designed for environmental scale and long-term brand presence.

The animation framework accounts for technical constraints of large-scale LED infrastructure—refresh rates, pixel density, and ambient light interference. Each animation maintains brand identity while remaining legible across drastically different viewing contexts, from close-range pedestrian traffic to highway sightlines.
